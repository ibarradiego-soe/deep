01

Why I built this concept

I've always been fascinated by how the mind works.

For years I've explored how psychological patterns influence the way people make decisions, build companies, and navigate uncertainty.

A few weeks ago I started building an AI tool designed to identify entrepreneurial archetypes and help founders make better decisions based on how they think.

The idea was simple: different founders process risk, conflict, and uncertainty in very different ways — yet almost no tools help them understand those patterns.

02

Connecting the dots

Around that time, I came across Andrew Wilkinson's post about Deep Personality.

So I ran my own profile through the system.

The result immediately resonated.

Not only because the analysis was surprisingly accurate, but because it confirmed something I had been seeing repeatedly while working with founders: many business problems are not strategy problems — they are self-awareness problems.

That realization changed my direction.

Instead of continuing with my original project, I began building a system inspired by the core ideas behind Deep Personality — but focused specifically on Spanish-speaking founders and entrepreneurs.

You can see the early beta here: sistemaemprendedor.io

Over the last few days more than 120 beta users have gone through the system, helping refine the psychological frameworks and insights behind it.

03

Why I might be the right person to build this

After running my own Deep Personality profile, something became very clear.

My profile shows high openness, strong pattern recognition, and an unusually high interest in understanding how people think and behave. It also highlights a mix of entrepreneurial drive and curiosity about psychological models and frameworks.

In other words, I sit at the intersection of two worlds that rarely meet:

  • building companies
  • studying how the mind works

That combination is precisely what a product like Deep needs.

Someone obsessed not only with building businesses, but with understanding the invisible mental patterns behind how they are built.

04

The next step

After testing the beta with founders, I realized something bigger.

Rather than building a parallel product, it made much more sense to imagine what Deep Personality could become if extended into the world of entrepreneurship.

So I decided to take the leap and build this concept — a vision of how Deep could evolve into a decision companion for founders and executive teams.

And to share that vision directly with Andrew.

05

The vision for Deep

Deep could evolve into a decision intelligence system for founders.

Most tools help leaders understand their companies.
Deep helps them understand the mind making the decisions.

The next phase would focus on five capabilities.

Founder decision profiles

Turn psychological analysis into decision maps for how founders process risk, uncertainty, conflict, and irreversible decisions.

Decision pattern memory

Track decisions over time and surface recurring patterns: bias, hesitation, risk misjudgment, and leadership conflict triggers.

Leadership dynamics mapping

Analyze how founders and executives differ in decision speed, risk tolerance, and conflict response.
Reveal structural sources of friction inside leadership teams.

AI decision companion

A system founders can consult during high-stakes moments: hiring, strategy shifts, partnerships, and negotiations.

Leadership infrastructure

Deep becomes a new layer of infrastructure for decision-makers.
Software that helps founders understand the psychology shaping their companies.

06

What this page is

This page is a concept.

But if built well, Deep could become something entirely new:

Self-awareness infrastructure for decision-makers.

Most founders spend years trying to improve their companies.

Very few spend time understanding the mind building the company in the first place.

Deep could change that.

And if we get it right, it might fundamentally change how founders think, decide, and build.
